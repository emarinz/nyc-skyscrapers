Iconic Skyscrapers

The Battle for the Tallest

From the late 1920s to the early 1930s, there was an intense competition to build the world’s tallest skyscraper in New York City. That competition resulted in the construction of two of the most iconic skyscrapers ever built, the Chrysler Building and the Empire State Building.

Photos by Lewis Hine of the construction of the Empire State Building

  • Chrysler Building
  • Empire State Building

The Chrysler Building was the first building in the world to rise higher than 1,000 feet. At its completion it overtook the Eiffel Tower as the world’s tallest man-made structure. It stood as the tallest building in the world from 1930 until 1931. It remains the tallest steel-framed brick building in the world.

The Empire State Building is the first building in the world to contain over 100 floors. Upon its completion in 1931, it surpassed the Chrysler Building as the world’s tallest building. It remained the tallest until the World Trade Center was completed in 1972 and now stands as the 30th-tallest in the world.
